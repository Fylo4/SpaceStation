The Lord's Prayer

Draft Written 4/23/2025 Edited 4/23/2025

Pray, then, in this way: Our Father in heaven, may your name be revered as holy. May your kingdom come. May your will be done on earth as it is in heaven. Give us today our daily bread. And forgive us our debts, as we also have forgiven our debtors. And do not bring us to the time of trial, but rescue us from the evil one.

Matthew 6:9-13, NRSVUE

I use this passage as a template for contemplative prayer. When I pray this way, I break it into 7 sections:

Our Father in heaven

I think about the oneness of God and the unity of God and all things.

May your name be revered as holy

(Or, "Hallowed be thy name"). I think about my reverence for God and rest in a place of divine love for a bit. I see God and all of God's creation as holy. I try to see God in everybody (as in Matthew 25), and see them as holy, too.

May your kingdom come. May your will be done on earth as it is in heaven

See my article on the Kingdom of Heaven. It is an affirmation that I am committed to making the earth more like divine paradise. It is a reminder for righteousness. I ask "What do I need to do today to help bring about God's kingdom on earth?", and "What do I need to be moving towards in the long-term?". And it is hope, that one way or another, the world will change to become heavenly.

Give us today our daily bread

Here I think about any immediate needs for myself or others, and pray that these needs are fulfilled.

Forgive us our debts

I think about anything I did wrong, anything I am guilty of. Just asking "what am I guilty of" will bring several instances in mind. I ask for forgiveness, repent, and think about how to avoid this in the future. This step is aimed at making me a better person over time.

As we also have forgiven our debtors

Think about any grudges I hold against others or anyone I need to forgive, and forgive them.

Do not bring us to the time of trial, but rescue us from the evil one

(or, "Lead us not into temptation"). Think about any sins or temptations that I am facing, either temptations that recur daily, or temptations I predict I will face at some point today. Commitment to doing what is right in those moments.

Gratitude

This isn't part of the Lord's prayer, I'm just describing how I pray now. After I finish with those 7 points I then take some time to pray about what I am grateful for.
